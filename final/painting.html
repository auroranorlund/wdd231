Painting of the Month: August 2025

L'aurore, Mer du Nord, Guillaume Vogels, 1877

Guillaume Vogels was a Belgian Impressionist painter who lived from 1836-1896. Due to a lack of personal record keeping and undated works, little is known about himself or his development as a painter. He was an apprentice to the Bellis Brothers, and his friend Périclès Pantazis introduced him to the works of French realist and impressionist artists, eventually teaching him to paint with palette knives. In 1870 he moved to Paris where he developed his personal style, and in 1880 he participated in the Paris Salon, marking a breakthrough in his career. While not much is known about L'aurore, Mer du Nord (meaning Dawn, North Sea), it is a beautiful depiction of the stillness of a simple dawn over the ocean.
